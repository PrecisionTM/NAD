Who It's For

NAD+ Decline Affects
More Than You Think.

NAD+ levels drop roughly 50% between ages 40 and 60 — directly affecting your energy, cognition, sleep quality, and how fast your cells age. These are the four most common symptoms of NAD+ decline:

Chronic Fatigue

Without sufficient NAD+, your mitochondria can't produce adequate ATP — leaving you running on empty regardless of how much you sleep.

Brain Fog & Memory

NAD+ is critical for neuronal repair and neurotransmitter function. Declining levels directly impair focus, memory, and mental processing speed.

Disrupted Sleep

NAD+ regulates SIRT1 — a key circadian clock gene. Low levels throw off your sleep-wake cycle, causing poor sleep quality and night waking.

Accelerated Aging

PARP1 and sirtuins — the body's primary DNA repair and longevity proteins — depend entirely on NAD+. Without it, cellular aging accelerates.

Honest Transparency

Who NAD+ Is Not For

We believe in clinical honesty. NAD+ therapy is not the right fit for everyone — and your safety always comes first.

Under Age 30

Your NAD+ levels are still naturally high. Therapy is designed for adults experiencing age-related cellular decline, typically accelerating after age 35–40.

Active Cancer or Malignancy

NAD+ promotes cellular energy and repair — which could theoretically support cancer cell proliferation. Your physician will screen carefully before prescribing.

Pregnancy or Breastfeeding

NAD+ therapy has not been studied in pregnancy or lactation. Safety in these populations has not been established.

Looking for Overnight Results

NAD+ works at the cellular level — results build progressively over weeks and months. If you want an instant fix, this program isn't for you.

Severe Kidney or Liver Disease

Patients with significant hepatic or renal impairment may need dose adjustments or may not be candidates. Your physician will evaluate your full history.

Unwilling to Self-Inject

NAD+ is administered via a small subcutaneous injection. If self-injection is a hard no, we'd recommend exploring our other oral-friendly programs instead.

NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell of your body. It serves as a critical cofactor in hundreds of metabolic reactions, most importantly in the electron transport chain that powers mitochondrial ATP production — your cells' primary energy currency. NAD+ also activates sirtuins (longevity-regulating proteins), fuels PARP enzymes responsible for DNA repair, and regulates circadian rhythm genes. By your 50s, NAD+ levels have typically dropped 40–50% from youthful levels, directly causing the fatigue, cognitive decline, poor sleep, and slower cellular repair that feel inevitable with aging — but aren't.

NAD+ has an excellent safety profile. It is a naturally occurring molecule in the body, and subcutaneous supplementation is well-tolerated by the vast majority of patients. Common mild side effects can include minor injection site redness, temporary flushing, or mild GI discomfort — especially early in treatment. These typically resolve within days. Your Precision Telemed physician will review your full health history before prescribing to confirm NAD+ is appropriate for you.

Research suggests consistent daily dosing may produce better sustained outcomes for energy, cognition, and cellular repair than infrequent high-dose IV sessions.
